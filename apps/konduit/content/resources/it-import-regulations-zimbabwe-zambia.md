Importing IT equipment into Zimbabwe and Zambia requires correct documentation, realistic lead times, and a clear understanding of customs processes.

## Zimbabwe

**Port of entry.** Hardware typically enters via Beitbridge (road) or Harare International Airport (air freight for urgent shipments).

**Documentation.** Commercial invoice, packing list, bill of lading or airway bill, and ZIMRA import documentation. OEM certificates of conformity may be required for certain equipment categories.

**Lead times.** Allow 10 to 18 business days from European dispatch, including customs clearance. Air freight reduces transit time but increases cost.

**Duty and VAT.** Import duty and VAT apply. We provide landed cost breakdowns in our quotes so there are no surprises.

## Zambia

**Port of entry.** Common routes include Dar es Salaam to Lusaka (road corridor) or direct air freight to Lusaka.

**Documentation.** ZRA requires commercial invoices, packing lists, and transport documents. SADC certificates of origin may reduce duty on qualifying goods.

**Lead times.** Allow 12 to 20 business days from European dispatch via road corridor. Air freight to Lusaka is faster but more expensive.

## General advice

- Confirm delivery address and recipient details before ordering
- Ensure someone is available to receive and sign for shipments
- Retain all customs documentation for audit and warranty purposes
- Build customs clearance time into project schedules
