In Southern African markets, discounted hardware is often available from local suppliers. The price difference can be significant. The warranty gap can cost more.

## What grey market means

Grey market hardware is sold outside the manufacturer's authorised distribution channels. It may be genuine product, but the manufacturer has not authorised its sale in that market. This typically voids warranty coverage.

## The real cost of no warranty

Without valid manufacturer warranty:

- Failed components must be replaced at full cost
- RMA processes do not apply
- Firmware updates and security patches may be unavailable
- Audit and donor compliance requirements may not be met

A laptop that fails 18 months into a three-year project cycle costs more to replace than the original saving.

## How to verify authorised supply

Ask your supplier for:

- Distributor name and authorisation
- OEM part numbers on the quote
- Warranty registration process after delivery
- Serial number documentation on delivery

If a supplier cannot provide these, the warranty position is unclear.

## Price is one variable

Total cost of ownership includes purchase price, warranty coverage, support availability, and replacement risk. Authorised supply through European distributors typically costs more upfront and costs less over the equipment lifecycle.
