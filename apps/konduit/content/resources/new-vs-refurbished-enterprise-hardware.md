The choice between new and refurbished enterprise hardware is not simply about budget. Warranty validity, support availability, and project requirements all factor in.

## When refurbished makes sense

Refurbished enterprise hardware from authorised refurbishers can offer strong value for:

- Non-critical workloads where downtime tolerance is higher
- Training and development environments
- Short-term project deployments with defined end dates
- Backup and disaster recovery capacity

Look for refurbishers who provide their own warranty and clear grading standards (Grade A, B, etc.).

## When to buy new

Buy new when:

- Manufacturer warranty and support are contractually required
- The hardware supports a production or revenue-critical workload
- Donor or tender specifications require new equipment
- You need the latest generation for software compatibility

## Warranty is the deciding factor

The critical question is not new versus refurbished. It is whether valid warranty and support exist for the specific unit. Grey market hardware, regardless of condition, typically carries no manufacturer warranty.

## Ask for documentation

Whatever you choose, request warranty terms in writing, serial numbers before delivery, and confirmation of the supply channel.
